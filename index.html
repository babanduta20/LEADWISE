About Us

Leadwise is a professional consultancy firm established in 2012 and duly registered in both Australia (ABN: 64803078970) and South Sudan (Registration No. 35957). We are a diverse team of experts with cross-sectoral experience, committed to equipping leaders in the Public, Private, NGOs, Development Agencies, civil society sectors with practical skills and strategic tools to enhance institutional effectiveness and impact.

We support organizations to strengthen performance, solve complex challenges, and implement tailored solutions that drive efficiency, profitability, and sustainable growth. Our work spans across business man- agement, strategic planning, leadership development, and organizational transformation ensuring our clients are equipped to thrive in dynamic environments.

  • Mission: To be a catalyst for innovative change and continuous improvement empowering people, strengthening human resource capacity, and building high-performing organizations and resilient institutions for sustainable impact.
  • Vision: To inspire, empower, and support leaders across business, government, and civil society to drive transfor- mative change. We work to align strategy, people, culture, structure, and systems to ensure organizations achieve their goals effectively and sustainably.
  • Our Core Values: At Leadwise, our work is guided by values we deeply believe in and consistently uphold:
    1. Integrity and Professionalism: We maintain the highest standards of ethics, accountability, and professional conduct in everything we do.
    2. Client Commitment and Partnership: We are dedicated to building strong, collaborative relation ships with our clients, grounded in trust, respect, and shared success.
    3. Continuous Improvement and Transformation: We strive for excellence through ongoing learning, innovation, and the pursuit of meaningful change

MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE

Leadwise is led by a dynamic, award-winning MBA professional and supported by a multidisciplinary team of seasoned experts, including educators, public administrators, researchers, transport and agricultural engineers, and ICT practitioners. Together, the leadership team shares a strong commitment to transfor- mational leadership and a common goal: to empower change agents and promote sustainable develop- ment.

Our governance approach is informed by diverse professional experiences across South Sudan, Australia, the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, Kenya, Uganda, and Rwanda. This international exposure strengthens our ability to deliver globally informed yet locally grounded solutions.

Leadwise operations are underpinned by a robust governance framework and adherence to international best practices. Our institutional policies include:

  • Protection from Sexual Exploitation and Abuse (PSEA) Policy
  • Environmental Policy
  • Gender Equality and GBV Code of Conduct
  • Health and Safety Policy
  • Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy
  • Conflict of Interest Policy
  • Child Protection Policy
  • Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E) Policy
  • Procurement Policy
  • Human Resources and Finance Policies
  • Organizational Code of Conduct
  • Board of Directors Oversight Procedures

    • These policies ensure integrity, accountability, transparency, and ethical conduct in all aspects of our work and partnerships.

HIGHLIGHTS OF SUCCESSFULLY COMPLETED PROJECTS

Women and Youth Leadership Programme (2024–2025)

Partner: UNDP, Office of the Vice President (Chair of Gender and Youth Cluster) A one-year leadership and internship program for 200 participants focused on capacity building, mentorship, and preparing youth especially women for leadership roles and employment across South Sudan.

Road Safety and Traffic Management Project

Partners: University of Juba, JICA (2022–2024) Designed and implemented a road safety and traffic management initiative targeting drivers and pedestrians. Trained 500 drivers in defensive driving and reached 10,000 residents through safety awareness campaigns.

Food Security and Livelihood Needs Assessments

Partner: Relief International (2024) Conducted multi-county assessments in Maban, Longechuk, and Melut to identify food security gaps, resilience needs, and livelihood opportunities, informing community-based interventions.

Market Assessment, Gender Analysis, and Food Security Baseline

Partner: USAID/RASS Activity (2022) Undertook assessments across 13 counties and 5 states, producing actionable insights on market systems, gender dynamics, and agricultural resilience.

WASH Baseline Survey

Partners: South Sudan Red Cross (Ikotos, 2020), Amref Health Africa (Maridi & Ibba, 2021) Evaluated maternal and child health, nutrition, and service coverage to inform community health strategies and programming.

National Education Assessment

Partners: UNESCO (2023) Supported the Humanitarian Needs Overview by assessing dispari- ties in education access, infrastructure, and policy implementation.

HR and Institutional Capacity Building Program

Partner: UNDP (4.5 Years), CSF (2019-2022) Delivered human resources and institutional strengthening programs across 68 ministries/agencies of the Government of South Sudan.

Peace and Reconciliation Program

Partner: Peace Canal (June–August 2023) Facilitated community-driven peacebuilding efforts in Jonglei and Lakes State through participatory dialogue and local leadership empowerment.

Project Evaluation Services

Partners: UN Women, Jonglei Civil Society Network, ZOA, CSF (2020–2023) Conducted multiple mid-terms and endline evaluations to assess impact, draw lessons learned, and enhance accountability for peacebuilding, women’s empowerment, and WASH interventions.

Child Protection and Safety Capacity Building

Partners: Ministry of Gender, Child, and Social Welfare – Jonglei State, UNMISS, UNHCR (2021–2022) Trained 240 children and multiple child protection actors; established local protection committees and strengthened referral systems for abuse prevention and child safeguarding.

Health WASH Assessment and Community Training

Partners: WHO, UNICEF, Life of Service Foundation, Ministry of Health (June–Dec 2023) Conducted WASH assessments and training in Bor, Duk, Twic East, and Akobo counties, focusing on hygiene promotion, water safety, and healthcare-associated infection prevention.
